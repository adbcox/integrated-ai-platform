# Document Retention and Deprecation Policy

## Purpose

This document explains how roadmap and planning documents should be retained, superseded, deprecated, or archived.

## Core rule

Documents should not silently remain active after their durable content has been normalized elsewhere.

## Document classes

### Canonical documents
These remain active and authoritative until explicitly replaced.

### Supporting documents
These remain useful but are not the primary authority.

### Historical / legacy documents
These are retained for provenance and context, but should not be treated as current truth.

## Required handling when a document is superseded

When a document is superseded:

- identify the new canonical replacement
- update the superseded document or surrounding index to say so
- avoid leaving two equally plausible current sources

## Archival rule

Do not delete useful historical planning material casually.
Instead:

- retain it as legacy/historical where appropriate
- ensure the canonical replacement is obvious

## Reader shorthand

The goal is not fewer documents.
The goal is fewer ambiguous documents.
